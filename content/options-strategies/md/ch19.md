Every structure so far has drawn a single shape with one or two profit zones. This final chapter of the advanced section stacks them. Picture taking two of the strategies you already know and placing them side by side on the same chart, so the payoff grows two separate peaks or two separate tables instead of one. That is what the **batman**, the **double fly**, and the **double condor** do. The batman bolts two ratio wings together for a wide, high-probability credit with naked tails on both sides. The double fly and the double condor place two defined-risk range structures around the money, betting that NIFTY makes a moderate move in either direction rather than sitting still. They look intimidating, but each is just two familiar pieces sharing one diagram.

## The batman

The **batman** gets its name from its shape: two profit peaks rising on either side of the money like the ears of a cowl, with a dip in the middle. You build it by combining a put ratio wing below the money and a call ratio wing above it, on the 28 July 2026 expiry.

| Leg | Action | Strike | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Buy | 24,050 | Put |
| 2 | Sell | 23,950 | Put (x2) |
| 3 | Buy | 24,050 | Call |
| 4 | Sell | 24,150 | Call (x2) |

The two sold pairs bring in more than the two bought legs cost, so the batman opens for a **net credit of about Rs 5,941**. Each ratio wing builds its own peak. The put wing peaks as NIFTY falls toward 23,950, and the call wing peaks as NIFTY rises toward 24,150, so the chart carries twin profit humps reaching up to a **maximum profit of Rs 22,191**. In the middle, around spot, the payoff dips between the two peaks.

The catch is the same naked tail you met in the ratio spread, except the batman carries one on each side. Below the lower **breakeven of 22,959** the extra sold puts run the loss down toward a very large figure, and above the upper **breakeven of 25,141** the extra sold calls run the loss up without any ceiling at all.

| Number | Value |
|---|---|
| Max profit | Rs 22,191 |
| Max loss | Unlimited |
| Breakevens | 22,959 / 25,141 |
| Probability of profit | 78 percent |
| Risk to reward | NA |
| Net credit | Rs 5,941 |
| Margin required | Rs 209,721 |

{{image: strat-batman.png | The NIFTY batman: twin profit peaks reaching Rs 22,191 on either side of the money, a dip between them around spot, and breakevens at 22,959 and 25,141 beyond which the naked ratio tails run away on both sides. | chart}}

:::warn
A batman has a maximum loss that is UNLIMITED. It holds extra sold calls above and extra sold puts below, so it is naked on both sides. Past the upper breakeven of 25,141 the loss climbs without a ceiling on a rally, and past the lower breakeven of 22,959 it deepens toward a very large figure on a fall. The exchange blocks about Rs 2.1 lakh of margin, the heaviest in this section, because two naked tails are more dangerous than one. The 78 percent probability of profit reflects the wide zone between the breakevens, not the size of either tail.
:::

The batman wins most of the time because NIFTY usually finishes somewhere between 22,959 and 25,141, a very wide band. But a single large move in either direction can hand back many quiet wins at once. It is a professional's tool for a range-bound view, not a beginner's first credit trade.

## The double fly

The **double fly** places two butterflies side by side, one centered below the money and one above it. Instead of a single tent over the current price, you get two tents flanking it, with a valley in between. It bets that NIFTY makes a moderate move in either direction rather than sitting still.

The two profit tents and their four breakevens tell the whole story.

| Component | Profit tent | Center |
|---|---|---|
| Lower butterfly | 23,495 to 23,805 | about 23,650 |
| Upper butterfly | 24,295 to 24,605 | about 24,450 |

Read the four **breakevens at 23,495, 23,805, 24,295 and 24,605** from the outside in. You profit inside the lower tent, from 23,495 to 23,805, and inside the upper tent, from 24,295 to 24,605. Between them, in the central valley around spot, you take your worst loss, and far outside both tents the defined wings cap the loss as well. The **maximum profit is Rs 10,072** at a tent peak, and because every leg is balanced and bought, the **maximum loss is only Rs 2,928**. It is fully defined-risk on both sides.

| Number | Value |
|---|---|
| Max profit | Rs 10,072 |
| Max loss | Rs 2,928 |
| Breakevens | 23,495 / 23,805 / 24,295 / 24,605 |
| Probability of profit | 24 percent |
| Risk to reward | 1:3.4 |
| Net credit | Rs 23,072 |
| Margin required | Rs 130,313 |

{{image: strat-double-fly.png | The NIFTY double fly: two profit tents, one peaking near 23,650 and one near 24,450, separated by a loss valley around spot, with four breakevens at 23,495, 23,805, 24,295 and 24,605 and a defined worst loss of only Rs 2,928. | chart}}

:::key
A double fly profits when NIFTY makes a moderate move either way, landing in one of the two tents, and loses if it sits still in the central valley or runs far past the outer wings. It pairs a defined, modest loss of Rs 2,928 against a larger reward of Rs 10,072, a clean 1 to 3.4 payoff, but the 24 percent probability of profit is honest about the catch: price has to land in a fairly narrow zone to win.
:::

## The double condor

The **double condor** is the double fly with flat tops. It places two condors side by side, again one just below the money and one just above, so each profit zone is a small plateau rather than a sharp peak. The trade-off for that wider sweet spot is a smaller reward.

| Component | Profit zone | Center |
|---|---|---|
| Lower condor | 23,807 to 23,991 | about 23,899 |
| Upper condor | 24,107 to 24,291 | about 24,199 |

The four **breakevens at 23,807, 23,991, 24,107 and 24,291** sit closer to the money than the double fly's, so this structure wants a smaller move. You profit on the lower table from 23,807 to 23,991 and on the upper table from 24,107 to 24,291, lose in the narrow central valley around 24,050, and lose again beyond the outer edges. The **maximum profit is Rs 2,675** and the **maximum loss is only Rs 575**, the cheapest worst case of the three structures here.

| Number | Value |
|---|---|
| Max profit | Rs 2,675 |
| Max loss | Rs 575 |
| Breakevens | 23,807 / 23,991 / 24,107 / 24,291 |
| Probability of profit | 16 percent |
| Risk to reward | 1:4.6 |
| Net debit | Rs 575 |
| Margin required | Rs 127,183 |

{{image: strat-double-condor.png | The NIFTY double condor: two flat-topped profit tables, one near 23,900 and one near 24,200, separated by a loss valley around spot, with four breakevens and a tiny defined worst loss of Rs 575. | chart}}

:::info
The double condor costs a net debit of only about Rs 575, which is also its maximum loss, so its margin of about Rs 1.27 lakh reflects the exchange blocking cash against the multi-leg position rather than any open-ended risk. Its 16 percent probability of profit is the lowest here, because the two narrow tables demand that NIFTY land in just the right place: a precise, cheap, low-odds bet on a small move either way.
:::

## How the three compare

| Strategy | Max profit | Max loss | Probability of profit | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Batman | Rs 22,191 | Unlimited | 78 percent | naked on both sides |
| Double fly | Rs 10,072 | Rs 2,928 | 24 percent | defined |
| Double condor | Rs 2,675 | Rs 575 | 16 percent | defined |

The three split into two camps. The **batman** is a high-probability credit trade that wins across a huge range but hides naked tails on both sides and the heaviest margin in the course. The **double fly** and **double condor** are fully defined-risk bets on a moderate move in either direction, with worst cases of just Rs 2,928 and Rs 575, but low odds because price must land in a fairly tight zone to pay.

:::fact
Notice how the reward and the odds trade off. The batman offers Rs 22,191 of profit at 78 percent odds, but only because it sells the naked tails that make that wide win zone possible. The double condor offers a 1 to 4.6 payoff at just 16 percent odds, with a worst case of Rs 575. There is no free lunch here: every wider win zone is paid for either in naked risk or in a smaller, less likely reward.
:::

:::tip
For a beginner, the double fly and the double condor are the safe members of this family. Their worst case is printed on the ticket, a few thousand rupees or less, and they teach you how stacked structures behave without exposing you to a runaway loss. Leave the batman until you are fully comfortable defending naked options on both sides at once, and never carry its twin tails through an event unhedged.
:::

You can build all three in **sandbox trading (analyzer mode in OpenAlgo)**, placing the legs and watching the twin peaks or twin tables appear on the orange line. Drag the modelled price into a tent and out into the valley to feel where each structure wins and loses, and on the batman, drag it past a wing to watch the naked tail take over. That hands-on feel is the best way to make these big stacked shapes intuitive.
