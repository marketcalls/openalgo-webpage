Most of the trades so far have been comfortable middle-ground bets, paying a few thousand rupees of risk for a few thousand of reward. This chapter goes to the opposite extreme. The **bullish butterfly** and the **bullish condor** are tiny, surgical bets that cost almost nothing and can pay many times their cost, but only if NIFTY lands in a narrow window at expiry. They are the cheapest structures in the whole course, with a maximum loss of barely a hundred rupees, yet they also carry the lowest odds, about one in ten. That combination, a cheap ticket on a long shot, is exactly what they are, and this chapter teaches them honestly as what they are good for and what they are not. All figures use real NIFTY option data captured on 26 June 2026, spot 24,056, lot 65, expiry 28 July 2026. Both structures are built entirely from calls.

## The bullish butterfly

A butterfly has three strikes and four legs, arranged so that you buy the wings and sell the body twice. For a bullish butterfly the whole structure is nudged just above the money, so it pays best if NIFTY drifts up to a precise target.

| Leg | Action | Strike | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Buy 1 | 24,050 | Call |
| 2 | Sell 2 | 24,150 | Call |
| 3 | Buy 1 | 24,250 | Call |

The two sold 24,150 calls collect premium, and the two bought wings at 24,050 and 24,250 cap the risk on both sides. The premiums very nearly cancel, leaving a **net debit of just Rs 120** for the whole lot. That tiny debit is also the entire maximum loss. You simply cannot lose more than Rs 120, no matter where NIFTY goes, because the wings box the structure in on both sides.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Max profit | Rs 6,380 |
| Max loss | Rs 120 |
| Breakevens | 24,042 and 24,254 |
| Probability of profit | about 10 percent |
| Risk to reward | 1 : 53 |
| Net debit | Rs 120 |
| Est. premium | Rs 96,333 |
| Total P&L now | about Rs 0 |
| Margin required | Rs 63,628 |

The **maximum profit is Rs 6,380**, earned only if NIFTY closes right at the body strike of 24,150 on expiry. That is the sharp peak of the tent. The risk to reward is a spectacular 1 : 53, because you stake Rs 120 to make as much as Rs 6,380. But read the probability of profit before you fall in love. It is only about 10 percent, because the profitable window is narrow, running roughly from the lower breakeven at **24,042** to the upper breakeven at **24,254**, and the full peak is reached at just one point.

Read the chart and the shape tells the whole story. Almost everywhere, far left and far right, the orange line lies flat at a loss of Rs 120, so close to zero it is hard to see. Only in the narrow zone around 24,150 does it spike up into a tall, thin peak. The two amber breakeven dots sit at 24,042 and 24,254, essentially at the outer wings, and the full Rs 6,380 is reached only if NIFTY pins 24,150 exactly. Step a little either way and the profit shrinks fast.

:::key
A bullish butterfly is a cheap, precise bet that NIFTY drifts up to a specific target, here 24,150, and sits there at expiry. You risk only Rs 120 to make up to Rs 6,380, a risk to reward of 1 : 53. The catch is the probability of profit, about 10 percent. The shape pays brilliantly when you are right about the exact landing spot, and quietly costs you a tiny amount when you are not.
:::

## The bullish condor

A **condor** is a butterfly with its peak stretched into a plateau. Instead of selling the same strike twice, you sell two different middle strikes, which widens the top of the tent into a flat zone. That gives NIFTY a small range to land in rather than a single point, at the cost of a lower peak profit.

| Leg | Action | Strike | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Buy 1 | 24,050 | Call |
| 2 | Sell 1 | 24,100 | Call |
| 3 | Sell 1 | 24,200 | Call |
| 4 | Buy 1 | 24,250 | Call |

Again the premiums nearly cancel, leaving a **net debit of just Rs 88**, which is the whole maximum loss. The two sold strikes, 24,100 and 24,200, form the edges of a flat profit plateau between them.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Max profit | Rs 3,162 |
| Max loss | Rs 88 |
| Breakevens | 24,040 and 24,254 |
| Probability of profit | about 10 percent |
| Risk to reward | 1 : 36 |
| Net debit | Rs 88 |
| Est. premium | Rs 96,366 |
| Total P&L now | about Rs 0 |
| Margin required | Rs 63,757 |

{{image: strat-bullish-condor.png | The NIFTY bullish condor, buying the 24,050 and 24,250 calls and selling the 24,100 and 24,200 calls. The orange line forms a flat topped tent at a maximum profit of Rs 3,162 between roughly 24,100 and 24,200, with breakevens at 24,040 and 24,254 and a near flat floor at a maximum loss of just Rs 88. | chart}}

Read the chart against the butterfly's. The condor's top is no longer a single spike but a short flat shelf, sitting at the **maximum profit of Rs 3,162** anywhere NIFTY finishes between about 24,100 and 24,200. The breakevens, at **24,040 and 24,254**, are similar to the butterfly's, but the reward is lower, Rs 3,162 against Rs 6,380, because you traded peak height for a wider landing zone. The probability of profit is still only about 10 percent, since even the widened plateau is a narrow target relative to where NIFTY might travel in a month.

:::info
The difference between a butterfly and a condor is the width of the bullseye. The butterfly pays the most but only at one exact strike. The condor pays less but across a small band of strikes. Choose the condor when you have a rough target zone rather than a single price, and the butterfly when your conviction about the precise landing point is strong.
:::

## The honest verdict on these long shots

These two structures are genuinely useful, but only if you understand what you are buying. The numbers tell the truth plainly.

| | Bullish butterfly | Bullish condor |
|---|---|---|
| Net debit and max loss | Rs 120 | Rs 88 |
| Max profit | Rs 6,380 | Rs 3,162 |
| Risk to reward | 1 : 53 | 1 : 36 |
| Prob. of profit | about 10 percent | about 10 percent |
| Breakevens | 24,042 and 24,254 | 24,040 and 24,254 |

The dazzling risk to reward, 1 : 53 or 1 : 36, is real but it is balanced by the slim 10 percent odds. About nine times in ten, NIFTY finishes outside the narrow window and you lose your tiny debit, a hundred rupees or so. About one time in ten it lands in the zone and you collect a multiple of that. This is not an income strategy and it is not a core position. It is a cheap, targeted lottery ticket on a specific landing spot, and it should be sized like one.

:::warn
Do not be seduced by the 1 : 53 risk to reward in isolation. The probability of profit is only about 10 percent, so the most likely single outcome, by far, is that NIFTY misses the window and you lose the small debit. These bets are cheap precisely because they rarely pay. Treat them as occasional, low-stake punts on a precise view, never as a reliable way to make money.
:::

There is one more honest wrinkle. Although the cash you can lose is only Rs 88 to Rs 120, the margin the builder blocks is about Rs 63,000, because the structure contains sold legs that the exchange margins on exposure. So while your real risk is tiny, you still tie up meaningful capital to hold the trade, which makes these poor choices for a small or fully invested account even though the loss itself is trivial.

:::tip
The right moment for a bullish butterfly or condor is when you have a clear target price and a clear expiry, for example you expect NIFTY to grind up to about 24,150 and stall by month end. Place the body at that target, keep the stake small because the odds are against you, and treat the rare big payoff as a bonus, not a plan. If you have no precise target, a defined-risk spread from the earlier chapters is the better bullish tool.
:::
